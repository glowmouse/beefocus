1 Beefocus
2 ========
3 
4 Beefocus is an Open Source Hardware/ Software Telescope Focuser
5 The goal of the project is to create a high quality DIY focuser that's
6 relatively easy to build and modify.
7 
8 It's similar in spirit to other open source focuser projects. For
9 example...
10 
11 - https://sourceforge.net/projects/arduinoascomfocuserpro2diy/
12 - http://www.scopefocus.info/arduino-focuser-2
13 - https://sourceforge.net/projects/arduinomoonlitefocuserclone/?source=directory
14 - https://github.com/aruangra/PnP-Focus
15 
16 Why start a new project?
17 ------------------------
18 
19 - **Originally, I needed a custom focuser solution for my telescope**.
20  I have a Celestron C11 with a Hyperstar. When I started this I
21  couldn't find a good out of the box solution.
22 - **I want to try using an ESP-8266 internet of things micro-controller
23  instead of a traditional arduino**. I've found that every wire you
24  get rid of simplifies your telescope setup and observing
25 - **I want to open-source the hardware design under a permissive license.**
26  I'm intrigued by the idea of open-source hardware. I'm including the
27  Eagle CAD files for the hardware under a Creative Commons 4.0 license.
28  The goal is to make builds as inexpensive and easy as possible.
29 - This project is a good personal learning experience.

36 Status
37 ------
38 
39 - I have a physical prototype. It's been field tested and
40  it worked almost flawlessly. The issues that I want to fix before
41  "Version 1.0" are tracked on the [Github Project Issues Page].
42 - The [Prototype ESP8266 Firmware] is posted.
43 - There is a Prototype INDI Driver. I need to refactor the INDI driver
44  once I'm finished making changes to the firmware and ask to have my driver
45  pulled into the [Main INDI Project].
46 - I've published a [Prototype Board Design]. I made my prototype using
47  a CNC circuit router. I've sent the board off to be professionally
48  fabricated, and I'm waiting for the results.
